Finding Community

In the summer of 2012, I set out for a walk from my home in East Flatbush, Brooklyn, with no particular destination in mind. There was no traffic on Clarkson Avenue when I set out that evening at 10 p.m. A few cabs scurried here and there but, for the most part, all was quiet. There was, however, this fleeting sense that something ominous needed to be unearthed, exposed, and addressed. I was beginning to take a deeper look at my community at that time, because I was fed up with the lack of cohesion on so many levels within it (and in others like it). A lack of cohesion in the sense that community members and actors in the community needed to realize that their individual plights are tied together, because without that understanding, the community members cannot act together to become the agents of change needed to maintain and move their communities forward. As I journeyed, I thought about trying to determine the factors that could really change the trajectory of people’s lives and our overall quality of life right there in the inner city of Brooklyn. How could we foster the kinds of healthy conditions within the inner city that allows for sustainability in our social, political, and economic ecosystem?
